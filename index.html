About Me

Hello, I am Dylan Morris

Software Developer. Piano Player. Swing Dancer.

Connecting with an audience is my driving factor, no matter the stage. I grew up in Pittsburgh, PA and moved to Nashville to pursue a career in music sharing songs that come from my heart with my voice and a piano. I received a Bachelor of Arts in Songwriting from Belmont University and continue writing and performing today.


I have supported my vision with day jobs ranging from juggling plates at the busiest restaurant in Nashville to performing live science demonstrations for budding explorers at the Adventure Science Center, and even widening the smiles on faces of students at Swing Dance Nashville as they practice their new skills and experience the joy of partner dance!


When Coronavirus rudely interrupted our our lives it pushed me to explore my curiosity of coding. I applied for the 6 Month Web Developer Bootcamp at Nashville Software School delighted to find joy in the creativity, teamwork, and architecture of writing code. I love how communities sprout around tech applications! I am interested in working on projects that make people feel like they are a part of something.

Holiday Road

Holiday Road is a travel brochure application for users to plan a trip to a national park and save their itenerary.


In this team project we collaborated to design our app starting with a wireframe and dependency graph. We were given personalities of potential users and designed our app with minimilism in mind. My teammate noticed that the user we were designing for had a preference for websites with white backgrounds so we designed our app to be similar.


My role was to retrieve the weather information from the API and connect it to the park selected by the user so that a weather forecast for the desired location could be viewed while planning your trip.
